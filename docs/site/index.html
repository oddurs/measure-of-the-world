The Measure of the World

A Technical History of the Royal Observatory

by Oddur Sigurdsson

A comprehensive technical history of the Royal Observatory, Greenwich, spanning nearly 350 years (1675–present). This book traces how astronomy transformed from an ancient discipline into a modern scientific enterprise—through precision measurement, mechanical ingenuity, and international cooperation. The narrative integrates rigorous mathematics, detailed instrument analysis, biographical context, and institutional history.
